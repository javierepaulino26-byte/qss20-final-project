## Project Overview
This project analyzes the relationship between institutional median family income and SAT performance across colleges and universities in the United States. The goal is to explore whether socioeconomic factors are associated with differences in standardized testing outcomes at the institutional level.

The analysis combines exploratory data analysis and regression modeling to identify patterns between income levels and SAT scores.

## Research Question
Is institutional median family income associated with average SAT scores across U.S. colleges and universities?

## Dataset
The dataset used in this project comes from the **U.S. Department of Education College Scorecard** dataset.

Source:  
https://collegescorecard.ed.gov/data/

Key variables used:
- `SAT_AVG` – Average SAT score of admitted students
- `MD_FAMINC` – Median family income of students attending the institution
- `REGION` – Geographic region of the institution

## Methods
The analysis was conducted using Python and includes:

- Data cleaning and preprocessing
- Exploratory data analysis
- Scatterplot visualization
- Boxplot comparison across geographic regions
- Linear regression analysis

Libraries used:
- pandas
- matplotlib
- seaborn


## Results
The analysis identifies a positive relationship between institutional median family income and average SAT scores. Institutions with higher median family income tend to report higher SAT averages.

Regional analysis also shows variation in SAT score distributions across different geographic regions.
